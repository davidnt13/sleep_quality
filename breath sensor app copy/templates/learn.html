Understanding Your Sleep Metrics

Track and understand your breath rate, apneas, hypopneas, and AHI to monitor sleep quality.

What Happens When You Sleep

Sleep is a complex process which allows for the regulation of tissues, organs, and systems throughout the body. It is important for maintaining your body's activity and is a necessary component of a healthy lifestyle. Disruptions in sleep can cause health problems such as high blood pressure, cardiovascular disease, and depression [1].

See below to learn more about different sleep-based components this tool focuses on:

Breathing Metrics

Breathing is a very important component of sleep quality analysis. Over the course of the sleep cycle, your respiration rate-- or your breath rate-- fluctuates, slowing down during deep sleep and speeding up during REM sleep. Understanding these changes can provide identification of irregularities such as apneas, hypopneas, and tachypneas [2].

Apneas & Hypopneas

Apnea: A full pause in breathing for 10 seconds or longer. These pauses during sleep can be indicative of sleep-related breathing disorders such as obstructive or central sleep apnea [2]. Obstructive sleep apnea is actually severely overlooked, with up to 80% of cases remaining underdiagnosed [3]. This makes tools like ours pivotal for ensuring proper sleep performance.

Hypopnea: A partial reduction in breathing during sleep that results in shallow breathing episodes. This is another indicator of sleep apnea disorders [4].

Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI)

AHI measures the number of apnea and hypopnea events per hour. It is a useful metric for diagnosing and measuring the severity of sleep apnea in patients [5]. The following categories are used to classify the severity of these apnea and hypopnea events:

  • Mild: 5–14 events per hour
  • Moderate: 15–29 events per hour
  • Severe: 30+ events per hour
